About the NH CPR Challenge

What Is the CPR Challenge?

The NH CPR Challenge is a bipartisan initiative by New Hampshire's five Executive Councilors to train as many Granite Staters as possible in Hands-Only CPR during EMS Week 2026 (May 17–23).

Each Executive Council district is competing to see who can train the most people. All trainings are completely free and take just 15–20 minutes. No prior experience is needed.

The goal: give every New Hampshire resident the confidence to act if they witness a cardiac arrest. You don't need to be a medical professional—you just need to know two steps.

For every minute that passes without CPR, a cardiac arrest victim's chance of survival drops 7–10%. When a bystander steps in immediately, they can double or triple the chance the person survives. Hands-Only CPR is simple, effective, and can be learned in minutes.

What Is Hands-Only CPR?

Hands-Only CPR is CPR without mouth-to-mouth breaths. It's recommended for use by people who witness an adult or teen suddenly collapse. It has just two steps:

Step 1: Call 911

If you see someone suddenly collapse, call 911 immediately (or direct someone nearby to call). Every second counts.

Step 2: Push Hard & Fast

Place the heel of your hand on the center of the person's chest. Push hard and fast at a rate of 100–120 compressions per minute. Don't stop until professional help arrives.

Tip: Push to the beat of "Stayin' Alive" by the Bee Gees—it's about 100 beats per minute, the ideal compression rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a real CPR certification?

No. This is a Hands-Only CPR awareness training. You'll receive a certificate of participation, but it is not an official CPR certification from the American Heart Association or Red Cross. If you need formal certification (for a job, etc.), we encourage you to take a full course.

Who can attend?

Anyone! There are no age restrictions or prerequisites. Hands-Only CPR is designed to be accessible to everyone.

How much does it cost?

Nothing. All trainings through the NH CPR Challenge are completely free.

How long does the training take?

Most sessions are 15–20 minutes. You'll learn the two steps, practice compressions, and be on your way.

Can I host a training?

Yes! Fire departments, businesses, churches, community organizations, and individuals are all welcome to host. Apply here.

What district am I in?

Check our interactive map to find your Executive Council district.

What if I can't make it to a training during EMS Week?

We encourage you to check out the American Heart Association's free online resources at cpr.heart.org to learn Hands-Only CPR on your own.
